Civil Matters

Criminal Matters

Criminal lawis the body oflawthat relates tocrime. It prescribes conduct perceived as threatening, harmful, or otherwise endangering to theproperty,health,safety, andwelfareof people inclusive of one's self. Most criminal law is established bystatute, which is to say that the laws are enacted by alegislature. Criminal law includes thepunishmentandrehabilitationof people who violate such laws.

Divorce & Maintenance

In India, divorce legally ends a marriage and can be sought on grounds like cruelty, adultery, or mutual consent under personal laws. Maintenance ensures financial support for the dependent spouse or children, determined by factors like income, needs, and standard of living.

Land Matters

In India, land matters cover ownership, transfer, and disputes over property. Governed by laws like the Transfer of Property Act and state-specific laws, these issues involve title verification, encumbrances, and boundary conflicts, resolved through revenue or civil courts.

Service Matters

In India, service matters relate to employment issues in public service, including appointments, promotions, transfers, disciplinary actions, and termination. Governed by rules like the Central Civil Services Rules and relevant acts, disputes are addressed by tribunals like the Central Administrative Tribunal (CAT) or courts.

Muncipal Matters

In India, municipal matters involve urban governance issues such as property tax, building permits, sanitation, waste management, and town planning. Governed by state-specific Municipal Acts, these matters are handled by municipal corporations or councils to ensure urban development and public welfare.

Education Matters

In India, education matters encompass issues like admission, fees, scholarships, staff appointments, and institutional regulations. Governed by laws like the Right to Education Act and university-specific statutes, disputes are handled by education boards, tribunals, or courts to ensure compliance and fairness.

Electricity Matters

In India, electricity matters include issues like power supply, billing disputes, outages, and illegal connections. Governed by the Electricity Act, 2003, and state regulations, these are addressed by electricity boards, regulatory commissions, or consumer grievance forums to ensure fair access and resolution.

Consumer Matters

In India, consumer matters relate to disputes over goods or services, including defects, deficiencies, unfair trade practices, or overcharging. Governed by the Consumer Protection Act, 2019, such issues are resolved through consumer forums or commissions to protect consumer rights and ensure fair practices.

Accident Claim Matters

In India, accident claim matters involve seeking compensation for injuries or damages caused by accidents, including road, work, or public transport accidents. Governed by the Motor Vehicles Act and other relevant laws, claims are filed through insurance companies or courts. Compensation is determined based on medical expenses, loss of income, and the severity of the injury or damage.

Loan & Settlement Matters

In India, loan and settlement matters involve disputes or agreements related to borrowing, repaying, or settling loans. Issues may include default, interest rates, foreclosure, and settlement of outstanding amounts. These matters are governed by laws such as the Indian Contract Act and the Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code. Disputes are resolved through negotiations, banks, or courts, where borrowers may seek loan restructuring or settlement to clear dues.

Insurance Matters

In India, insurance matters involve issues like claim disputes, policy terms, and premium payments. Governed by the Insurance Act and regulated by IRDAI, these are resolved through insurance companies, ombudsman services, or courts.

Company Matters

In India, company matters include issues related to company formation, management, compliance, shareholder rights, mergers, and disputes. Governed by the Companies Act, 2013, these matters are handled by regulators like the Ministry of Corporate Affairs (MCA) and addressed through company boards or legal forums.

Agreement, Deed & Registration

In India, agreements are legally binding contracts, deeds are formal documents conveying rights or obligations, and registration is the process of officially recording these documents with authorities for legal validity under the Registration Act.
